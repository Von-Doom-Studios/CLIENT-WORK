# 09 - POSTING PLAYBOOK

Mechanics for writing X posts and replies that earn engagement. Applied before any post or reply ships.

## What the algorithm rewards (2026)

| Action | Weight |
|---|---|
| Back-and-forth conversation (we reply to a reply) | 150x |
| Reply to our post | 27x |
| Repost | 20x |
| Like | 1x |
| External link in post body | ~0 (suppresses reach) |

A post with 5 replies we engage back with is worth more than a post with 500 likes. Optimize for replies.

## The 18-minute rule

A post loses half its reach in the first 18 minutes. The first line is the whole game. If the first line doesn't earn the second, the post is dead.

## Audit checklist (apply before every post or reply)

1. Does the first line stop the scroll without context?
2. Is there one idea, not two?
3. Is every word doing work? Can I cut 20% without losing meaning?
4. Is there any jargon a normal X reader won't recognize? If yes, replace or cut.
5. Is the CTA a single plain URL or action verb (not "click here / learn more / DM us")?
6. If it's a reply, is the value-add in the first sentence, with the brand mention (if any) last?
7. Are there any em dashes or hyphens used as em dashes? Rewrite the sentence if yes.
8. Are external links out of the post body? (Links go in the first self-reply.)

Failing any one of these = rewrite, not ship.

## Worked example (before/after)

**Before:**

> An AI agent can now book a real flight on Brij. It searches live airline inventory, pays in USDC over the x402 protocol, and returns a real PNR. No card. No airline account. No IATA accreditation.

Fails audit on jargon (PNR, IATA accreditation) and on stacking three ideas.

**After:**

> An AI agent can now book your flight. It searches the same airlines you would, finds the cheapest seat, pays for it, and sends you the confirmation. You don't need an account anywhere. travel.brij.fi

Passes: concrete event in plain language, one idea, every clause earns its place, CTA is a plain URL.

## Reply rules

Replies are the highest-leverage thing we do as a small account.

1. Be in the first 3-5 replies. After ~30 minutes a reply is buried.
2. Add a thing the author didn't say (a counter-point, a sharper example, a real number). Never "Great post!" or emoji.
3. Match the author's register (technical, casual, terse). Mismatched tone reads as bot.
4. Value-add first sentence. Brand mention (if any) goes last, never first.
5. Polite disagreement out-performs agreement, when we have a real point. Use sparingly.
6. Drop the URL only when it's clearly relevant. A reply that's secretly an ad is worse than no reply.

## Post-and-engage

A post is not done at publish. The first hour decides reach.

- Reply to every comment, fast. The author replying back is the 150x signal.
- If a post hits 5+ replies in the first hour, reply to your own post with a follow-up to extend its life.
