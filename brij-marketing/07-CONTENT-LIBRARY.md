## Voice rules

The voice is **builder-confident**: declarative, technical when it earns its keep, dry when humor lands, plain when humor doesn't. The reader is a developer or operator who recognizes real engineering work and tunes out marketing performance.

What this means in practice:

- **Lead with the customer's problem and the proof, not with a punchline.** Most posts open with the thing that broke or the thing that shipped. Wit follows. Wit never leads.
- **Specificity over abstraction.** Real flows. Real receipts. Real numbers. Real failure modes. Never "transforming," "revolutionizing," "reimagining."
- **One idea per post.** If a post needs two ideas, it's two posts or it's a thread.
- **Sentence case on X is fine. All-lowercase as a permanent affectation is not.** Lowercase is used sparingly, for asides or sub-lines, not as a substitute for craft. Default to sentence case.
- **No em dashes.** Hyphens or restructure.
- **Banned phrases:** "excited to announce," "thrilled," "we're so proud," "gm fam," "revolutionizing," "game-changing," "the future of," "stay tuned," "you guessed it," "your wallet wallet" and similar repetitive-word stunts.
- **No empty hooks.** No "let me tell you a story," no "here's what nobody is talking about."
- **LinkedIn:** sentence case, first person, founder voice, 3-5 short paragraphs, one sharp insight per post. No corporate filler. No hashtag stacks.
- **X length:** target 240 characters for a punchy single, longer when a receipt or proof needs the space.

### Voice anchors (good vs bad)

Bad: "your wallet wallet. your actual smart wallet."
Good: "A flight booked, paid in USDC, settled in seconds. No card, no airline account, no IATA setup. travel.brij.fi is live."

Bad: "gm. we're brij now."
Good: "Espresso Cash is now Brij. Same team, broader scope: the payments layer for AI agents. The first thing it does is book your flight."

Bad: "we're revolutionizing wallets with AI agents."
Good: "Most wallets hold money. Ours spends it on behalf of software, on policies you set. That's the wedge."
